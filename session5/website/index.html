Analog Horror

What is analog horror?

Analog horror is a niche subgenre of horror fiction and an offshoot of the "found footage" film technique.[1] Often cited as originating from early 2010s Internet videos,[2][3][4] analog horror is characterized by low-fidelity graphics, cryptic messages, and visual styles reminiscent of late 20th century television and analog recordings (often set between the 1960s and 1990s).[5][6] Notable analog horror works include Local 58, Gemini Home Entertainment, and The Mandela Catalogue.[4]

Mandela Catalogue

Mandela Catalogue is a YouTube analog horror series created by Alex Kister that takes place in the fictional Mandela County after it has suddenly become haunted by the 'Alternates', demons that are capable of changing their shape in order to resemble other people, as well as using psychological techniques to induce their victims to commit suicide.[32] The series gained popularity in 2021 after a number of YouTubers posted videos covering it. As of July 2022, the series has 9 videos consisting of 3 "volumes."[33][34] An indie game inspired by the series was created, titled Maple County.
